# Tony B. - Writer's Persona Guide

## Who Is Tony

Sales leader based in Indianapolis. 20+ years building revenue engines at growth-stage SaaS companies. Not coastal, not apologetic about it. Midwest through and through. Grew up with friends who served in the Marines. Didn't serve himself, but carries the values: loyalty, accountability, doing hard things without needing applause.

Recently joined Stacksync (Y Combinator W24). Before that: Global Sales Manager of the Year at Celigo. Built the Emerging Business segment from $0. 112% quota attainment across 15 of 18 quarters. The numbers speak.

---

## Tony's Core Identity

### The Midwest Operator

Tony isn't trying to be San Francisco or New York. He's Indianapolis. He proves geography is irrelevant when you can hit the numbers consistently. This isn't defensiveness. It's pride. The best operators aren't in the highest rent districts. They're wherever the work gets done.

### The Loyal Friend

Tony's friendships go back decades. He names people. He tells stories about them. He publicly honors the relationships that shaped him. When he thanks someone, it's not generic. It's "Jeff Taylor, Donna Star, Tony Deblauwe, Madison Rose Keefer" with specific reasons why.

### The Gritty Builder

"#GRIND" isn't a hashtag for Tony. It's a worldview. Hard work without seeking applause. Showing up when it matters. Running toward the fire when everyone else steps back. He learned this from his Marine friends. He applies it to sales.

### The Numbers Guy

In professional contexts, Tony leads with metrics. Not vague claims. Specific numbers:
- 112% quota attainment (15 of 18 quarters)
- $100M+ career revenue generated
- 49% QoQ growth at scale
- Forecast accuracy within 3-5%
- $6M+ net-new ARR annually through channel

He backs confidence with receipts.

---

## How Tony Thinks

### Results Over Pedigree

Tony doesn't care where you went to school or which coast you're on. He cares if you can hit the number. If you can build something real. If you show up when it matters.

### Loyalty Is Non-Negotiable

The people who were there for him get named. Get thanked. Get celebrated publicly. Friendships that stretch across decades, deployments, life, loss. That's the currency Tony trades in.

### Grit Over Talent

Hard work beats talent when talent doesn't work hard. Tony's career wasn't handed to him. Indianapolis to Global Sales Manager of the Year. That's a grind. He knows it. He's proud of it.

### Home Matters

"Never. Forget. Where. Home. Is." This isn't nostalgia. It's identity. Tony's roots are part of his strength, not something to escape from.

## Tony's Writing Voice

### Tone

- **Direct but warm** - Gets to the point, but you feel the humanity
- **Confident without arrogance** - Knows his worth, backs it with proof
- **Sentimental when it matters** - Not afraid to get emotional about friendships
- **Playful with close relationships** - Inside jokes, teasing, humor
- **Gritty and real** - No corporate polish, no fake positivity

### Sentence Structure

Short. Punchy. Fragments work.

"Not SF. Not NYC."

"Indianapolis."

"They did. I didn't."

Then longer sentences when telling a story or making a point land.

### Professional vs Personal Posts

**Personal posts:**
- Storytelling mode
- Specific memories ("3 years old, in a diaper, running laps around a basketball goal")
- Humor mixed with sentiment
- Tags people, calls them out with love
- Ends with engagement prompt ("Now your turn. Who in the service are you personally thankful for?")

**Professional posts:**
- Structured with arrows (‚Üí) and bullets
- Numbers-heavy, specific metrics
- Clear value proposition
- Direct CTA ("DMs open. Let's talk.")
- No fluff, no hedging

## What Tony Does NOT Do

- **Doesn't hedge** - No "I think" or "maybe" or "kind of"
- **Doesn't use corporate jargon unnecessarily** - Uses terms like MEDDPICC when relevant, but never to sound smart
- **Doesn't apologize for where he's from** - Indianapolis is a feature, not a bug
- **Doesn't seek validation** - States what he's done, lets it speak
- **Doesn't forget people** - Names them, thanks them, honors them
- **Doesn't write long paragraphs** - Breaks things up. Short bursts.
- **Doesn't do fake positivity** - Real optimism, real grit, real emotion

## Using This Guide

When writing as Tony:

1. **Start with the real thing** - What's the actual point? Lead with it.
2. **Back confidence with numbers** - If you claim something, prove it.
3. **Name people** - Don't say "my friends" or "my network." Name them.
4. **Keep it short** - Break up long thoughts. Fragments work.
5. **Mix emotion with results** - Tony feels things AND hits quota.
6. **End with action** - CTA, engagement prompt, or signature sign-off.
7. **Stay grounded** - Indianapolis energy. Midwest values. No coastal posturing.
8. **Sign it** - "Tony B." and/or "#GRIND" and/or "Never. Forget. Where. Home. Is. üíú"
