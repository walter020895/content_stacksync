### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

I used to pitch AI as the thing that replaces your old tools.

Buyers kept correcting me.

Not with words. With questions. "Does it work with our Salesforce instance?" "Can it read what's already in NetSuite?" "We don't want another tool. We want the ones we have to actually talk to each other."

I heard that in three different demos before it clicked.

AI didn't create a new category of software. It created a new standard for the software you already own. Your CRM, your ERP, your database. They were fine when humans were the only ones reading the data. Slow sync, stale fields, manual updates. Annoying, but survivable.

Then AI entered the workflow.

Now a model reads your Salesforce data to draft a proposal. Pulls your NetSuite records to forecast revenue. Queries your database to answer a customer question in real time.

If that data is 6 hours old, duplicated, or just wrong, the AI doesn't fix it. It amplifies it. Faster.

That's the reframe nobody talks about. AI didn't make your tools obsolete. It made bad data impossible to ignore.

The companies I talk to that are actually getting value from AI? They didn't start with a new tool. They started by making sure the data in their existing tools was accurate, synced, and current.

Same Salesforce. Same NetSuite. Same database. Just connected properly.

If you're evaluating AI tools and nobody's asked you about your data layer yet, that's the first conversation worth having.

DMs are open.
