### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Freshworks.

Four CRMs that look nothing alike.

Every one started the same way: frustration.

Benioff spent 13 years at Oracle and quit because he believed enterprise software shouldn't require a six-month installation. Halligan watched outbound marketing dying and built HubSpot around the bet that inbound would replace it. Pipedrive's founders were running a sales team in Estonia and couldn't find a CRM simple enough for their own reps.

Databases follow the same playbook.

CockroachDB was built by ex-Google engineers who saw what Spanner could do and thought every company deserved a database that good.

ERP gets wilder.

Intuit exists because Scott Cook watched his wife struggle to balance a checkbook at their kitchen table. Workday was Dave Duffield's answer after Oracle's hostile takeover of his company PeopleSoft. SAP was five IBM engineers whose project got cancelled. They left and built it themselves.

And then there's Chennai. One city in India produced Zoho, Freshworks, and Chargebee. Zoho built 45 apps for less than one Salesforce license and never took outside funding.

I sell into teams that run these systems every day. When you understand how they were built and why, you start to see where they hit limits.

That's usually when the good conversations happen.

DMs are open if you nerd out on this stuff too.
