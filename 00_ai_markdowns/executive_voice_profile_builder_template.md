## How This Document Works

**This template is designed to be executed alongside a raw interview transcript.**

You will receive two files:
1. **This template** â€” the structure and instructions for extracting a personality profile
2. **A transcript** â€” a natural, conversational interview (not a rigid Q&A)

**Your job:** Read the transcript carefully, then fill out every section of this template by extracting, interpreting, and *inferring* from what was said. The interview is intentionally loose and conversational â€” it does not follow a fixed list of questions. The interviewer lets the person talk naturally, follows threads, and creates space for honesty. This means:

- **Answers won't be labeled.** The person won't say "my core belief is..." â€” they'll tell a story, and the belief will be embedded inside it. Your job is to find it.
- **Not every section will have a direct answer in the transcript.** Some sections require inference â€” connecting two things they said in different moments, reading emotional subtext, or noticing what they avoided.
- **Repetition is signal.** If they circle back to the same idea, phrase, or story more than once, that's a core element. Flag it.
- **Energy shifts are signal.** When the transcript shows them getting more animated, going longer, or getting quieter â€” that's meaningful. Note where those shifts happen and what triggered them.
- **Contradictions are signal.** If they say one thing early and something slightly different later, don't resolve it â€” capture the tension. That's where their most authentic voice lives.
- **Casual asides are gold.** The throwaway comments, the jokes, the "I don't know why I'm telling you this" moments â€” these often carry more voice truth than the polished answers.

### Processing Instructions

When filling this template from a transcript, follow this sequence:

1. **First pass â€” Read the full transcript without taking notes.** Get the feel of the person. Notice your gut impression.
2. **Second pass â€” Highlight and extract.** Pull exact quotes, recurring phrases, stories, beliefs, emotional moments, and anything that reveals how they think (not just what they think).
3. **Third pass â€” Fill the template section by section.** For each section:
   - Start with what's directly stated in the transcript
   - Then add what's implied or inferable
   - Then note what's missing or unclear (mark with âš ï¸)
   - Use direct quotes from the transcript wherever possible â€” wrapped in quotation marks with brief context
4. **Fourth pass â€” Write the inference sections (Part 9).** This requires you to step back and interpret the whole picture. What patterns emerged? What wasn't said? Where is the gap between how they see themselves and how they actually came across?
5. **Final pass â€” Complete the Quick-Reference Voice Card (Part 10).** This should be the distillation of everything above into an at-a-glance tool.
