### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

One served food at a football stadium with $150 in her pocket.
One still wears a $30 engagement ring from a Turkish market.
One started babysitting at 12 to pay for a degree nobody in her family had earned before.

They run HubSpot, Canva, and ServiceNow.

Yamini Rangan grew up in a 350-square-foot apartment in Tamil Nadu. Fewer than 8% of her engineering class were women. She flew to the U.S. alone at 21. Now she's CEO of HubSpot. The year she had to lay off 500 people, she took a $1 salary.

Melanie Perkins was rejected by over 100 investors. She was 19, in Perth, one of the most isolated cities on earth. She learned to kitesurf to get one meeting. Canva is now worth $42 billion. She's giving most of it away.

Gina Mastantuono quit her job and gave up her apartment to move across the country for her boyfriend. He ended things in her empty living room. She didn't go back. She traveled alone for six months. Now she's President of a $200 billion company.

Amy Hood has said she felt "deeply uncomfortable and unqualified" in every role she ever held. She's been Microsoft's CFO for 13 years. When she started, it was a $300 billion company. Today it's $3 trillion.

Ruth Porat's father fled Vienna at 16 on Kristallnacht. Lost his entire family. Never finished high school. His daughter became the most powerful woman on Wall Street, then brought financial discipline to Google. She created a fellowship in his name at Stanford.

Robin Washington grew up in blue-collar Detroit. English major turned #6 CFO in the entire S&P 500. She co-founded Black Women on Boards because the qualified women were already there. The world just didn't know where to look.

These are the women I study.

I don't care about the titles. I care about what they walked through.

And y'all, this list keeps me going.
