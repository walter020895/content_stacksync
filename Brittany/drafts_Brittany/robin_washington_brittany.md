### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

When Salesforce needed a new CFO, Marc Benioff didn't launch a search.

He looked around his own boardroom. She'd been sitting there for twelve years.

I needed to know who earns that kind of trust. So I went and read everything.

Robin Washington grew up in Lincoln Park, Michigan, a blue-collar suburb of Detroit. She entered the University of Michigan as an English major, pre-law, and somewhere in the middle pivoted to accounting and finance. She once called that pivot the moment she learned to "get comfortable being uncomfortable."

She spent eleven years as CFO of Gilead Sciences. Ranked number one in all of biotech. Number six among every CFO in the S&P 500.

In 2013, she joined Salesforce's board. Chaired the audit committee. Became Lead Independent Director. For twelve years, she was Marc Benioff's quiet strategic advisor.

In 2024, the outgoing CFO Amy Weaver stepped down. Months later, she became CEO of Direct Relief, the fifth-largest nonprofit in the United States.

And her replacement, Robin, had co-founded Black Women on Boards in 2020. What started as an 18-person Zoom call became a network of 200 women, with more than 30 placed on public company boards. Only 4% of S&P 500 board seats are held by Black women.

But y'all, here's the part I keep coming back to.

Salesforce committed to giving away 1% of its equity, 1% of its product, and 1% of employee time on the same day it incorporated in 1999. Four people in a one-bedroom apartment. Before a single customer or dollar of revenue.

And I love that when Benioff needed someone to run it all, he chose the woman who'd been building that same kind of purpose her entire career.

That's who I want to learn from.
