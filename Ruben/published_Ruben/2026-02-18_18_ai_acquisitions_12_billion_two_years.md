### FULL POST (using Hook C)

18 AI acquisitions. $12 billion. Two years.
Salesforce's biggest bet wasn't on agents.

It was on data.

Since 2023, Salesforce has made 18 AI-related acquisitions totaling over $12 billion. The headlines focused on Agentforce, their autonomous agent platform. The one Benioff calls "the future of digital labor."

But follow the money.

Informatica: $8 billion. Data catalog, integration, governance.
Own Company: $1.9 billion. Data protection and recovery.
Zoomin: $450 million. Unstructured data management.
Waii: Natural language to SQL.
Momentum: Conversational data from voice and video.

That's the data foundation. $10.4 billion. About 75% of every dollar they spent on AI.

The agent layer? Tenyx, Convergence.ai, Airkit.ai, Qualified, Bluebirds. Roughly $2 billion combined.

Five to one. Data over agents.

And even after spending $12 billion, only 8% of Salesforce's 150,000 customers have adopted Agentforce. Benioff himself admitted the speed of innovation exceeded the speed of adoption.

The Informatica deal tells the real story.

In 2024, Salesforce tried to buy them for $10 billion. Investors revolted. The stock dropped 7%. The deal died.

Informatica lost a third of its value over the next year.

Salesforce came back in 2025 and closed it for $8 billion. They wanted this company badly enough to try twice.

That's not a coincidence. That's conviction. The biggest CRM company in the world decided data infrastructure was worth more than everything else on their AI shopping list combined.

If you're building AI into your business, follow the same ratio. Not the model. Not the agent. The data underneath.
