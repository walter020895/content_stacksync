### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

Every AI company starts with a pitch deck.
We started with a support ticket.

The same question. Ten different customers. Same week.

"Can Stacksync tell me why this record didn't sync?"

Not "give us AI." Not "we need a copilot." Just: tell me what went wrong, without making me dig through logs.

We pulled the data. The top 10 support questions all had the same shape: customers troubleshooting sync issues that the system already had enough context to explain.

It was all there.

Error logs. Conflict history. Field mapping rules. The answers were already inside the platform. Customers just needed a faster way to surface them.

So we built it.

Not an AI wrapper. Not a chatbot bolted onto a dashboard.

An AI layer that reads your sync configuration, your error patterns, and your field mappings, then explains what happened and what to do next.

No prompt engineering required from the user.
No "ask me anything" interface.
Just direct answers to the questions customers were already asking support.

The entire AI roadmap came from one spreadsheet: the top 10 questions our support team answered every week.

I see founders building AI features because investors expect it. Because competitors announced it. Because the market narrative demands it.

We built ours because ten customers independently asked for the same thing in the same quarter.

That is the only signal worth following.

Build from what customers ask for. Everything else is noise.
