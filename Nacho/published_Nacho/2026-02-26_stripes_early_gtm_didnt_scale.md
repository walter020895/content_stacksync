### FULL POST (as published)

---

Stripe's early GTM didn't scale.

Patrick and John Collison flew to customers' offices and installed their product live. No trial link, instead: "We can have you running in minutes."

That built a $100B+ company.

To be honest, most B2B teams get this backwards:

They optimize for scalable. They run the follow-up sequence. But the Collisons understood: if you hand someone a link, you lose 80% of them. When you sit across from them and make it work in 20 minutes, you have a real customer.

7 lines of code. That was the entire Stripe integration. Not a slide about infrastructure. Not a roadmap. A working product, done in the room.

There's a name for what they did: Collison installation. And it's the reason Stripe got its first 100 customers before anyone else figured out payments was a solvable problem.

The GTM lesson isn't "don't scale." It's that friction kills more deals than price. Every step between "I'm interested" and "I'm using this" is a place where you lose them.

The best sales motion is the one that makes the gap disappear.

That's it.
