### FULL POST (using Hook A)

Every startup I've seen die had the same problem.

Not product. Not funding. Not even a bad idea.

Speed.

They had strategy decks. Alignment meetings. 90-day roadmaps. Notion boards with color-coded priorities.

And almost zero shipped output per week.

I've watched startups burn months of runway perfecting positioning while a competitor with half the team was already talking to customers.

The competitor's product was worse. Didn't matter. They were in the room. Learning from real users. Iterating while the other team was still aligning.

To be honest, every time I meet a struggling founder I ask one question: what did you ship last week, and who outside the company asked for it?

Two parts. The answers tell me everything.

Ship ugly. Talk to customers. Fix in public. Repeat.

The startups that survive don't have better strategy.

They have faster loops.

That's it.
