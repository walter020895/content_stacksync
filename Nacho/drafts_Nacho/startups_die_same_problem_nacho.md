### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

Every startup I've seen die had the same problem.

Not product. Not funding. Not even a bad idea.

Speed.

They had 90-day roadmaps and Notion boards with color-coded priorities. Bi-weekly alignment meetings. Every artifact of a team that's busy but not building.

And almost zero shipped output per week.

To be honest, I've watched startups burn months of runway perfecting positioning while a competitor with half the team was already talking to customers.

The competitor's product was worse. Didn't matter. They were in the room. Learning from real users. Iterating while the other team was still aligning.

The startups that survive don't have better strategy. They have faster loops. Ship ugly, talk to real customers, fix in public. That's the whole loop.

Every time I meet a struggling founder, I ask two questions: what did you ship last week? And who outside the company asked for it?

The answers tell me everything.

That's it.
