### FULL POST (using Hook A)

---

Salesforce bought Informatica. Fivetran and dbt announced a merger. IBM acquired Confluent for $11 billion.

Three data infrastructure deals in a single year. Same thesis.

The data market is compressing. Too many vendors doing the same thing. Too many point solutions pretending to be platforms.

The consolidation just started. And it's already brutal.

Look at what they bought. Not AI models. Not chatbot frameworks.

Data infrastructure.

Every deal is the same bet: whoever owns the data layer wins the AI race.

To be honest, I've seen this play out at every company I've worked with. The ones struggling with AI don't have a model problem. They have a data problem. Dirty inputs, fragmented systems, no single source of truth. You can't fix that with a better prompt.

The companies that win with AI won't have the best models. They'll have the best data.

Clean data. Scoped AI. Platform-level integration.

That's the stack that survives compression. Everything else gets acquired or dies.
